<p><strong>Vision</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>To nurture students who are future-ready digital learners</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>To develop teachers who are designers and facilitators of meaningful tech-mediated
learning experiences</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Mission</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>ICT Department Students Outcomes:</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Global Citizens: </strong>Students who exercise cyber wellness
to use technology safely and responsibly, demonstrate digital citizenship
that respects cultural diversity and global perspectives, and leverage
technology to engage with environmental and social issues both locally
and internationally.</p>
<p><strong>Lifelong Learners:</strong> Students who are digitally literate,
self-directed learners who embrace responsibility for their digital learning
journey, reflect critically on their use of technology, and demonstrate
curiosity and pride in exploring new digital tools and platforms.</p>
<p><strong>Innovative Thinkers:</strong> Students who use technology to understand
and respect different perspectives, generate creative digital solutions,
explore multiple technological pathways, and evaluate and refine their
ideas using digital tools to create meaningful innovations.</p>
<p>
<br><strong>Confident Leaders: </strong>Students who understand their digital
strengths, express themselves confidently through various technological
platforms, collaborate effectively in digital environments, and inspire
peers whilst co-constructing knowledge in digitally-empowered learning
communities.</p>
<p><strong><u>ICT Literacy Programme</u></strong>
</p>
<p>ICT literacy programme designed to nurture our students into digitally-empowered,
future learners and innovators. It focuses on developing students' Digital
Literacy and Technological Skills (DLTS) through engaging, age-appropriate
activities that build confidence and competence in using technology as
a tool for learning, creating, and problem-solving. Students will explore
various digital platforms, learn basic coding concepts, develop multimedia
creation skills, and understand digital citizenship principles including
online safety and responsible technology use.

</p>
